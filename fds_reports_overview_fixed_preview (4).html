The Financial Data Store Framework

FDS provides the foundational intelligence required to sustain financial, operational, and research decision-making. This framework organizes reports into 8 groups so users can move from question to report with precision.

8 Reporting Groups

Why This Training Exists

Most users do not begin with a report name. They begin with a question.

💰
How much is left?
📋
What hit my budget?
👤
Are payroll charges correct?
🔍
What is still open?
📊
Is this award overspent?
🗓
What needs cleanup before close?
This training helps users choose the right report by business purpose, not by alphabetical list.
Framework

How to Choose the Right Report

Start with the question. Then choose the report.

1
Identify the Question
Know the business need before opening FDS.
2
Find the Group
Match the question to one of the 8 reporting groups.
3
Start at Summary
Begin with the summary report for context and overview.
4
Drill to Detail
Move to detail only when the summary raises a question.
💡 Golden Rule: summary reports give context. Start high level, then drill down.

01
Group One

Budget & Financial Review

What's my budget position?

Group 01

Budget & Financial Review

Use this group when you need a high-level financial answer.

Use this group if you want to know…

  • How much budget remains?
  • How this year compares with last year
  • Which expense categories are driving spend
  • Where revenue posted
  • Whether a chartstring is over or under plan
Best Starting Reports
· Consolidated Operating Business (COB)
· Chartfield Summary Statement
· Project Summary Statement
· Department Summary Statement
Best Used When
Monthly review · Leadership meetings · Variance analysis
Primary Roles
Department administrators · Finance managers · Leadership
Why It Matters
This is the strongest starting point for general financial health and available balance review.
02
Group Two

Sponsored Projects & Research

Grant tracking, burn rate, and receivables

Group 02

Sponsored Projects & Research

Use this group when the question is tied to sponsored funding performance or compliance.

Use this group if you want to know…

  • How much award funding remains
  • Whether spending aligns with the budget category plan
  • What the project burn rate looks like
  • Whether receivables or billing issues exist
  • Whether the award is trending toward overspend
Best Starting Reports
· Sponsored Project Financial — Summary by Budget Category
· Sponsored Project Financial Report — Detail
· Sponsored Project Financial — Summary by Natural Account
Best Used When
Monthly grant review · PI meetings · Pre-closeout review
Primary Roles
Research administrators · Department finance teams · PIs
Why It Matters
Sponsored projects require tighter controls, stronger monitoring, and clearer budget-category visibility.
03
Group Three

Payroll Reports

Salary charges, distributions, and fringe

Group 03

Payroll Reports

Use this group when the question is about people, pay, or fringe.

Use this group if you want to know…

  • Who was paid on a chartstring
  • Whether salary was charged to the correct project or department
  • What fringe benefits posted
  • What changed between payroll periods
  • Which employees hit a unit or project
Best Starting Reports
· Payroll Summary Report
· Payroll Detail Report
· Fringe Benefit Report — Summary
· Department Employee Report
Best Used When
Monthly payroll reconciliation · Cost transfer research · Staffing review
Primary Roles
Finance managers · HR support staff · Department administrators
Why It Matters
Payroll is often the largest spend category and needs clear employee-level traceability.
04
Group Four

Procurement Reports

Open commitments, vouchers, and P-Card activity

Group 04

Procurement Reports

Use this group when the question is about purchasing activity, encumbrances, or payment execution.

Use this group if you want to know…

  • What purchase orders remain open
  • Which P-Card transactions posted
  • Whether vouchers are paid or still in process
  • What encumbrances are tying up budget
  • Which procurement items need follow-up
Additional Reports in This Group
· Open Procurement Transactions
· Voucher Payment Detail — Exportable
· SAPO Budget vs Encumbrance Report
Best Used When
Monthly close · Commitment review · AP follow-up
Primary Roles
AP staff · Procurement staff · Department administrators
Why It Matters
This group shows what has been committed, what has been charged, and what still needs action.

      Group 06

      Non-Sponsored Projects

      Use this group for internal projects that are not sponsored awards.

      Use this group if you want to know…

      • How gift, start-up, or discretionary projects are tracking
      • Whether internal project funding is still available
      • How project life spending compares with plan
      • Whether capital or special initiative activity is on pace
      • Which categories are driving project spend

      · Project Summary Statement
      · Expense Summary by Project
      · Chartfield Summary Statement

      Gift review · Start-up monitoring · Internal initiative tracking
      Primary Roles
      Department finance managers · Dean's Office teams
      Why It Matters
      Non-sponsored funds still need disciplined project-life monitoring, even without sponsor rules.
      07
      Group Seven

      Cash / Fund Balances

      Liquidity, fund position, and cash management

      Group 07

      Cash / Fund Balances

      Use this group when the answer depends on cash, fund balance, or liquidity position.

      Use this group if you want to know…

      • What the current fund balance is
      • Whether a unit has cash available
      • Where cash shortfalls or overdrafts exist
      • How balance sheet position is changing
      • Which funds need closer monitoring
      Best Starting Reports
      · COSTAR Summary Statement
      · Balance-oriented fund reports
      · Chartfield Summary Statement
      Best Used When
      Cash monitoring · Fund balance review · Operational planning
      Primary Roles
      Finance leadership · Department operations teams
      Why It Matters
      Available budget and available cash are not always the same. This group protects against that blind spot.
      08
      Group Eight

      Fiscal Year-End / Close

      Cleanup, accruals, encumbrances, and readiness

      Group 08

      Fiscal Year-End / Close

      Use this group when the question is about readiness, cleanup, or close discipline.

      Use this group if you want to know…

      • What remains open before close
      • Which encumbrances should be reviewed or cleared
      • Where accrual support may be needed
      • Which transactions still require cleanup
      • Whether close-related balances make sense
      Best Starting Reports
      · PO Open Commitment
      · Open Procurement Transactions
      · Transaction-level reconciliation reports
      Best Used When
      Month-end close · Fiscal year-end close · Audit prep
      Primary Roles
      Finance teams · AP teams · Leadership reviewers
      Why It Matters
      A clean close depends on disciplined review of commitments, open items, and unexplained balances.
      Summary

      Core Principles for Choosing FDS Reports

      1
      Start With the Question
      Choose the report that answers the business need, not the report with the most familiar name.
      2
      Start High, Then Drill Down
      Use summary reports first. Use detail reports only when you need transaction-level evidence.
      3
      Know the Reporting Group
      Each group reflects a different management purpose: budget, payroll, procurement, grants, cash, or close.
