How to Choose the Right Report

Start with the question. Then choose the report.

1
Identify the Question
Know what business need you're answering before opening FDS
2
Find the Group
Match your question to one of the 8 reporting groups
3
Start at Summary
Begin with the summary report for context and overview
4
Drill to Detail
Move to detail reports only when the summary raises a question
💡 Golden Rule: Summary reports give context — always begin at the high level before drilling down into transaction detail.

01
Group One

Budget & Financial Review

What's my budget?

Group 01

Budget & Financial Review

Best used when you need to know: How is this ChartString, department, or project performing financially?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • What's the budget for this project? How much is left?
  • How much have we spent this year compared to last year?
  • Are certain expense categories being spent faster than others?
  • What do we spend the most on?
  • How much did we earn in revenue?
Best Starting Reports
· Project Summary Statement
· Consolidated Operating Business (COB)
· Sponsored Project Financial Report – Summary by Budget Category
· Chartfield Summary Statement
Best Used When
Monthly Project Review · Budget Monitoring & Planning · Leadership Meetings
Primary Roles
Department Administrators · Finance Managers · Leadership
Why It Matters
The strongest starting category for overall financial health and high-level performance review.
02
Group Two

Sponsored Projects & Research

Grant tracking, burn rate, and receivables

Group 02

Sponsored Projects & Research

Best used when you need to know: How is this sponsored award tracking against budget, spending, and receivables?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • How much funding remains on the sponsored project?
  • Are there unusual or inappropriate charges on the award?
  • What is the project burn rate?
  • Are there receivables or sponsor-related financial issues to review?
  • Is the Indirect Cost allocation correct for my SAPOs?
Best Starting Reports
· Sponsored Project Financial – Summary by Budget Category
· Payroll Detail Report
· SAPO Budget vs Encumbrance Report
· Project Reporting Jump Page
Best Used When
Monthly · Quarterly · PI Meetings
Primary Roles
Grant Managers · Department Administrators · PIs · Post-Award Staff
Why It Matters
Critical for research stewardship, compliance, and PI support.
03
Group Three

Payroll Reports

Salary charges, allocations, and fringe

Group 03

Payroll Reports

Best used when you need to know: What payroll charges posted, for whom, and how were they allocated?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • What payroll charges posted, for whom?
  • How were salary charges allocated across ChartStrings?
  • Are fringe calculations correct?
  • What is the total labor cost for this department or project?
Best Starting Reports
· Payroll Detail Report
· Payroll Summary Report
· Department Employee Report
· Fringe Benefit Report – Detail & Summary
Best Used When
Payroll Review · Monthly · Quarterly
Primary Roles
Grants Managers · Department Administrators · HR Officers · Payroll Coordinators
Why It Matters
Payroll is often the largest expense area — precision here is non-negotiable.
04
Group Four

Procurement Reports

Open commitments, P-Card, and vouchers

Group 04

Procurement Reports

Best used when you need to know: What purchasing activity or open commitments are still affecting my budget?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • What purchase orders or commitments are still open?
  • What was charged on the P-Card?
  • What procurement activity is still affecting my available balance?
  • Which voucher payments have been processed?
Best Starting Reports
· Purchase Order Open Commitment
· P-Card Drillback
· SAPO Budget vs Encumbrance Report
· Voucher Payment Detail – Exportable
Best Used When
Monthly · Quarterly · Fiscal Year-End
Primary Roles
AP Staff · Procurement Coordinators · Business Managers
Why It Matters
Open commitments are often the hidden reason available balances appear lower than expected.
05
Group Five

Detailed Transaction Review & Reconciliation

Validate the books behind the balances

Group 05

Transaction Review & Reconciliation

Best used when you need to know: What exactly posted during the period, and does it reconcile?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • What exactly posted during the period?
  • Which transactions hit this account or ChartString?
  • Do the transactions reconcile?
  • Which charges, journals, or vouchers need review?
  • Which report should I use for monthly transaction review?
Best Starting Reports
· Trial Balance by Account Detail
· Trial Balance by Chartfield
· Payroll Detail
· Chartfield Detail Statement · P-Card Drillback
Best Used When
Monthly Close Preparation · Weekly · Quarterly
Primary Roles
Finance Staff · Reconciliers · Senior Business Staff
Why It Matters
This is the proof layer behind summary balances — essential for validating the books and catching coding errors early.
06
Group Six

Non-Sponsored Project Reports

Internal, gift, and start-up project monitoring

Group 06

Non-Sponsored Reports

Best used when you need to know: How is this non-sponsored project performing, and are there any balance or overspending concerns?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • How much revenue did we earn?
  • How much did we spend this month on supplies?
  • What is the remaining balance?
  • Is this project over budget or heading toward overrun?
  • What transactions posted to this project?
Best Starting Reports
· Non-Sponsored Project Financial Report – Summary
· Non-Sponsored Project Financial Report – Detail
· Project Reporting Jump Page
· Project Cost Summary-Overrun Report
Best Used When
Monthly Financial Review · PI Meetings · Budget Planning · Department Spending Overview
Primary Roles
Department Administrators · Finance Managers · Operations Leaders
Why It Matters
Separates internal project monitoring from sponsored reporting — supports better operational stewardship.
07
Group Seven

Cash / Fund Balances

Liquidity, overdraft, and cash position

Group 07

Cash & Fund Balance

Best used when you need to know: What is the cash position, and are there any fund balance or reconciliation issues?

Use this group if you want to know…

  • What is the cash position?
  • Are there fund balance or overdraft issues?
  • Do the bank balances reconcile?
  • How much cash is available?
Best Starting Reports
· Cash Balances Summary
· Cash Balances Detail – Exportable
· Fund Balance-Overdraft Reports
· Bank Account Reconciliation
Best Used When
Monthly Reconciliation · Balance Health Review · Cash Review
Primary Roles
Finance Managers · Division Finance Staff · Oversight Teams
Why It Matters
Supports liquidity awareness and identifies negative balances or fund stress early.
08
Group Eight

Closing the Fiscal Year

Accruals, encumbrances, and cleanup · Late April–June

Group 08

Closing the Fiscal Year

Best used when you need to know: What still needs year-end review, accrual treatment, or cleanup before close?

Use this group for…

  • Confirming which POs need to be rolled over
  • Review overdrafts before close
  • Identifying accrual items for Non-PO vouchers, Concur, P-Cards
  • Verifying encumbrances are treated correctly
Best Starting Reports
· Fiscal Year-End Encumbrance Report
· Closed Purchase Orders & Requisitions Reports
· FYE COB Summary Operating Statement
· FYE Concur Expense Detail Accrued · FYE P-Cards Accrued
Best Used When
Fiscal Year-End · Late April – June
Primary Roles
Finance Staff · AP Staff · Year-End Coordinators · Leadership Reviewers
Why It Matters
Year-end is a distinct control point — ensures outstanding items are treated correctly and the close process is supported with clean, defensible reporting.
