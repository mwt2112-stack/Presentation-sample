How to Choose the Right Report

Start with the question. Then choose the report.

1
Identify the Question
Know what business need you're answering before opening FDS
2
Find the Group
Match your question to one of the 8 reporting groups
3
Start at Summary
Begin with the summary report for context and overview
4
Drill to Detail
Move to detail reports only when the summary raises a question
💡 Golden Rule: Summary reports give context — always begin at the high level before drilling down into transaction detail.

01
Group One

Budget & Financial Review

What's my budget?

Group 01

Budget & Financial Review

Primary Question

How much have we spent, what’s already committed, and how much is left?

  • What’s our original budget?
  • How are we tracking this year compared to last year?
  • What has actually posted this month and fiscal year?
  • What is still committed but not yet fully spent?
  • How much budget is still available, and what percent have we used?
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Department administrators · finance managers · leadership
Key Fields to Watch
Original Budget · Previous FY YTD · Previous Actual · Current FY YTD · Current Estimate · Month Actuals · FY Actuals · Commitments · Budget Balance Available · Percent Used
Why This Matters
This is the clearest starting point for understanding budget, year-over-year movement, current activity, and remaining available balance.
Group 02

Sponsored Projects & Research

Primary Question

How is this sponsored award performing?

  • Remaining award funding and available balance
  • Burn rate, commitments, and spending health
  • Questionable or inappropriate award charges
  • Receivables and SAPO-related issues
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Grant managers · department administrators · PIs · post-award staff
Key Fields to Watch
Project Budget · Project to Date · Fiscal Year to Date · Encumbrance · Total Actuals and Commitments · Budget Balance Available
Why This Matters
This is the control center for research stewardship, sponsor compliance, and credible PI support.
03
Group Three

Payroll Reports

Salary charges, allocations, and fringe

Group 03

Payroll Reports

Primary Question

What payroll charges posted, for whom, and how were they allocated?

Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Grant managers · department administrators · HR officers · payroll coordinators
Key Fields to Watch
Employee name · salary charges · fringe charges · pay period · labor distribution
Why This Matters
Payroll is often the largest expense area, so allocation accuracy and timing discipline are essential.
04
Group Four

Procurement Reports

Open commitments, P-Card, and vouchers

Group 04

Procurement Reports

Primary Question

What purchasing activity or open commitments are still affecting my budget?

  • What purchase orders or commitments are still open?
  • What was charged on the P-Card?
  • What procurement activity is still affecting my available balance?
  • Which voucher payments have been processed?
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
AP staff · procurement coordinators · business managers
Key Fields to Watch
PO status · open commitment · vendor · voucher activity · P-Card transactions
Why This Matters
Open commitments are often the hidden reason available balances look lower than expected.
05
Group Five

Detailed Transaction Review & Reconciliation

Validate the books behind the balances

Group 05

Transaction Review & Reconciliation

Primary Question

What exactly posted during the period, and does it reconcile?

  • What exactly posted during the period?
  • Which transactions hit this account or chartstring?
  • Do the transactions reconcile?
  • Which charges, journals, or vouchers need review?
  • Which report should I use for monthly transaction review?
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Finance staff · reconcilers · senior business staff
Key Fields to Watch
Transaction date · amount · account code · journal source · supporting detail
Why This Matters
This is the proof layer behind summary balances and the best line of defense against coding errors.
06
Group Six

Non-Sponsored Project Reports

Internal, gift, and start-up project monitoring

Group 06

Non-Sponsored Reports

Primary Question

How is this non-sponsored project performing?

  • How much revenue did we earn?
  • How much did we spend this month on supplies?
  • What is the remaining balance?
  • Is this project over budget or heading toward overrun?
  • What transactions posted to this project?
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Department administrators · finance managers · operations leaders
Key Fields to Watch
Revenue · expense activity · remaining balance · overrun indicators · project-level transactions
Why This Matters
This provides disciplined monitoring for internal projects without conflating them with sponsored reporting.
07
Group Seven

Cash / Fund Balances

Liquidity, overdraft, and cash position

Group 07

Cash & Fund Balance

Primary Question

What is the cash position, and are there any fund balance issues?

  • What is the cash position?
  • Are there fund balance or overdraft issues?
  • Do the bank balances reconcile?
  • How much cash is available?
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Finance managers · division finance staff · oversight teams
Key Fields to Watch
Cash balance · overdraft exposure · reconciliation status · fund position
Why This Matters
This supports liquidity awareness and helps surface negative balances or fund stress early.
08
Group Eight

Closing the Fiscal Year

Accruals, encumbrances, and cleanup · Late April–June

Group 08

Closing the Fiscal Year

Primary Question

What still needs year-end review, accrual treatment, or cleanup before close?

  • Confirming which POs need to be rolled over
  • Review overdrafts before close
  • Identifying accrual items for Non-PO vouchers, Concur, and P-Cards
  • Verifying encumbrances are treated correctly
Best Starting Reports
Primary Roles
Finance staff · AP staff · year-end coordinators · leadership reviewers
Key Fields to Watch
Open encumbrances · accrual items · rollover decisions · close cleanup status
Why This Matters
Year-end is a control point. Clean review here supports a defensible close and stronger reporting integrity.
